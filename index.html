10 Core Tips for New Players

Quick Tips
01
Catch Instead of Kill

Catching Pals grants 3–5× more XP than killing them. Always carry plenty of Pal Spheres — weaken first, then throw.

02
Night XP Bonus ×1.2

The in-game night (roughly 8 PM – 6 AM) gives a 1.2× XP multiplier. Schedule your grind sessions after dark for maximum efficiency.

03
Base Location Is Everything

Build your main base near a dual Iron/Coal ore cluster to eliminate most hauling time. The area north of the Bamboo Forest is ideal for early game.

04
Every Pal Needs a Bed

Each working Pal must have a dedicated bed or its morale tanks and it will stop working. Beds required = number of working Pals. No exceptions.

05
Activate Fast Travel Anchors First

The moment you enter a new region, find and activate all fast travel anchors. This lets you warp back instantly after dying or returning to base.

06
Type Matchups Are Critical

Fire beats Grass/Ice; Water beats Fire; Electric beats Water/Dragon; Ground beats Electric; Dark beats Neutral. Always bring the right type for tower runs.

07
Dungeons Reset Weekly

All dungeons reset every Monday. The most efficient legendary blueprint rotation: Snow Tundra Dungeon → Volcano Ruins → Desert Labyrinth. Run with a party for speed.

08
Mount Pals Speed Up Exploration

Voltmane is the fastest land mount (speed 120); Frostchant is the top flying mount. Prioritize leveling one of each as early as possible.

09
Breeding Optimizes IVs & Passives

Breeding transfers passive skills and IVs from both parents. Use the passive inheritance system to build second-generation Pals far stronger than wild catches.

10
Watch Out for Heat and Cold Damage

Volcanic and snow regions deal continuous environmental damage. Don't push deep into either biome without the matching resistance armor set crafted first.

Breeding Tips & Mechanics

System Guide
🧬
Passive Slots Are Fixed at 4
Each Pal can hold up to 4 passive skills. When breeding, the game randomly samples from the combined pool of both parents' passives. Stack the key traits on one parent first to maximize odds of a clean 4/4 result.
🎂
Cake Is the Bottleneck
Each breed requires one Cake. Craft Cakes at the Cooking Pot using Flour (×5), Red Berries (×8), Milk (×7), Eggs (×8), and Honey (×2). Set up dedicated farms for these ingredients early to avoid breeding bottlenecks.
🥚
Egg Incubator Temperature Matters
Each egg type has an optimal temperature range. Placing a heat source or cooling unit next to the Egg Incubator speeds up hatching. Warm Eggs placed in a hot environment hatch significantly faster.
♻️
Breed Back for Trait Purity
If a bred offspring has 3 of 4 target passives, breed it back against a parent that holds the missing trait. Each breeding cycle improves the odds of hitting a full 4/4 passive set.
📊
IVs Also Transfer
Individual Values (IVs) for HP, ATK, and DEF also inherit from parents. Use Pals with high IVs as breeding stock — check stats in the Pal Status screen and filter for top percentile rolls.
⚡
Legend Passive Is Rare
The Legend passive only appears on Alpha Pals and certain boss-tier Pals. It cannot be bred from scratch — you need at least one parent that already carries it. Farm Alpha spawns in high-level regions to source it.

Beginner's Survival Guide

Just spawned on Palpagos and already overwhelmed? Don't sweat it. Follow these steps in order and you'll go from punching trees to running a fully staffed base without the frustration.

World Settings First

Before you even hit Play, tweak your world settings. Set Item Weight to 0.5×, Stamina Drain to 0.5×, and Death Drop to "Non-Equipped Items Only." These aren't cheating — they let you focus on catching and building instead of dying on your way back to your corpse.

First Five Minutes

Don't start swinging at trees. Your first job is to locate the nearest Gryphon Statue and activate it as a fast travel point — these are scattered everywhere and are your lifeline for later. Then grab enough Wood and Stone with your fists to craft a Basic Workbench from the quick-build menu. That unlocks everything else.

Pro Tip: Collect around 10 Pal Ore Shards (the little blue crystals glowing on the ground) as you explore. These are the core ingredient for crafting Pal Spheres, and you'll need a stack before you can even attempt your first catch.
First Pals to Catch

Not all starter Pals are equal. Here's who to prioritize and why:

🐗 Boar (Rushoar)
Worker · Priority: High
Partner skill headbutts ore nodes for way more ore than you can swing for. Also drops extra Pal Ore Shards. Grab 2–3 of these immediately.
🐒 Green Monkey (Verdash)
Logging · Priority: High
Exceptional logging ability and the partner skill pulls out an assault rifle to help in fights. Best early combat + work Pal in one package.
🐑 Cottoncot
Ranch · Priority: Medium
Drop it in a Ranch and it produces Wool passively. Wool is required for Pal Beds and cloth armor — get one as early as possible.
🦊 Foxspark
Ranch · Priority: Medium
Ranch it for a steady supply of Pal Spheres, including a small chance at higher-quality balls once it's upgraded to 4-star.
Catching Tip: Whittle a Pal's HP into the red zone, then approach from behind — capture rates are significantly higher from the rear. Base spheres only capture Pals under level 10, so don't try to yeet a level 25 Digtoise on day one.
Where to Build Your First Base

Base placement is the single most important early-game decision. Bad terrain means buildings won't place correctly. Here's what you're looking for:

CriteriaWhy It Matters
Flat, open groundStructures simply won't place on slopes — you'll waste materials attempting it
Near metal ore nodesYou'll need constant ore flow once you hit the metal tools era
Near water sourceWatering Pals need nearby water; also required for some farm structures
Defensible approachRaid events come from fixed directions — a narrow entrance means fewer entry points to defend
Recommended Starter Location: The flat area behind the waterfall on the right side of the starting plateau (approx. coords −234, 187). Three cliffs form natural walls with only one narrow entry point, and there's a hot spring behind the waterfall that slowly restores Pal Sanity — a free SAN recovery station before you can build a Spa.
Build Order

Once you've placed your Pal Box (which defines your base boundaries), follow this construction order to get a stable operation going as fast as possible:

PriorityStructureWhy
1stBeds (player + Pal)Save point + Pal rest — required for game saves and Pal stamina recovery
2ndFeed BoxPals feed themselves automatically when stocked with food — prevents work stoppages
3rdBerry Farm + Planting PlotSustainable food source; removes berry-foraging from your daily routine
4thLogging SiteLogging Pals auto-produce Wood; stops manual chopping sessions
5thMining SiteSame deal for Stone and Ore — assign a Rushoar or Digtoise here
Survival Basics

Campfires are non-negotiable at night. Without one, the Cold status will bleed you out steadily after dark. A bed isn't just rest — it's the only way to manually save the game. Make it a habit: every time you log off, sleep in a bed. Death drops your unequipped items, and a corpse run with no fast travel saved nearby is a painful way to lose progress.

Shortcut Keys Worth Memorizing: Press R to command your active Pal to attack your current target. Number keys 1–5 throw Pal Spheres to deploy Pals directly. These two keybinds alone dramatically increase your combat and capture efficiency.
When Are You Ready to Move On?

Don't rush the main story. You're stable and ready to push further when you have 5–6 Pals with dedicated jobs running in the background, a reliable food supply from your berry farm, and stable Wood + Ore output from your automated sites. At that point, your base runs itself and you can explore without constantly running back to micromanage.

Type Matchups & Combat Pals

Palworld's combat is built around a 9-type weakness system. Understanding it turns a frustrating boss fight into a clean one-sided stomp. Here's everything you need to build a winning team.

The Full Type Chart

There are 9 elemental types in Palworld. Each type has one or more weaknesses — and some dual-type Pals stack those weaknesses for up to 4× damage. Memorize the chart below before any Tower run.

Dual-Type Warning: Pals with two types inherit both weaknesses. Orserk (Electric/Dragon) is weak to both Ground and Ice — and since Ice hits both types, it deals 4× damage. Always check a boss's full type listing before selecting your lineup.

A well-rounded combat team has defined roles. Here's the standard framework:

Top Combat Passive Skills

Passive skills are what separate a decent Pal from a god-tier one. These are the best combat passives and what they do:

Legend (+20% ATK/DEF/Speed) Ferocious (+30% ATK, −5% DEF) Swift (+30% Move Speed) Runner (+20% Move Speed) Muscle Head (+30% ATK, −50% Work Speed) Calm & Collected (+10% ATK)
Optimal Combo: Legend + Ferocious + Swift + Runner gives your Pal explosive burst damage with the mobility to dodge mechanics. For pure output, swap Swift/Runner for Muscle Head + Calm & Collected — but that build is very squishy and requires tight positioning.

🦕 Relaxaurus

⚡ Taifeng

Advanced Combat Tactics

Don't stand there watching your Pals fight — you're a combatant too. Supplement with ranged weapons: archery headshots on a boss's weak spots (usually the head or back) deal bonus damage. When a boss telegraphs a big AoE, recall your Pal instantly, duck behind cover together, then redeploy after the animation ends.

The mount mount mount trick is real: mounting and dismounting gives a brief invincibility window that can be used to dodge otherwise undodgeable mechanics. It feels cheesy, but it's intended behavior. Use it on Tower Boss ultimate attacks.

Aggro Management: Press R to manually direct your Pal to target the boss — don't let it waste time on summoned minions. Meanwhile, you fire your weapons into the boss's back. Split aggro = more total DPS output and fewer Pal knockouts.

Base Building · 14 min read
Full Base Automation

Once you stop manually chopping trees and start building a proper production pipeline, Palworld clicks. Here's how to set up a genuinely hands-free base from scratch — and how breeding supercharges every worker in it.

Run Two Bases, Not One

Post-2026 patch, a single base can't realistically supply all your resource needs. The dual-base setup is optimal: Base #1 (Main Camp) handles crafting and living space in a defensible location — the waterfall area behind the starting plateau works great. Base #2 (Mining Outpost) goes to the southernmost point of the map, "Forgotten Shore," where tree density and ore density are 1.7× higher than anywhere else on the map.

Every Pal Box is a fast travel node. Put one at each base and you can teleport between them instantly to collect finished materials.

Anubis is the GOAT: The highest Handiwork level in the game (Lv4), plus Mining and Transport. One Anubis replaces three separate specialized Pals. Prioritize catching one in the desert ruins once you're Lv30+.
The Breeding System Explained

To breed, place one male and one female Pal in a Breeding Farm (unlocked at Tech Level 19), then drop a Cake in the attached chest. After a cooldown, an Egg appears — incubate it to hatch your offspring. Simple setup, massive depth.

Offspring randomly inherit passives from both parents. The probability distribution is: 40% chance to inherit 1 passive, 30% for 2, 20% for 3, and only 10% to hit a perfect 4-passive set. So expect to breed multiple rounds to get your ideal result.

Cake Recipe: Flour ×5, Red Berries ×8, Milk ×7, Eggs ×8, Honey ×2. Set up dedicated ranch Pals producing each ingredient automatically to avoid breeding bottlenecks.
The Gene Stabilizer — Game Changer

Added in the 2026 update: the Gene Stabilizer forces a breeding result to inherit 100% of the father's passives. This means you breed one perfect 4-passive father, then pair it with any mother, and every single offspring comes out with those four passives. No more RNG walls.

To get it: complete the full side-quest chain for the Black Market dealer in the northwest corner of the map, inside the "Rusted Canyon" area. The chain is long but completely worth it — finish it before you start serious breeding.

Condensing and the 4-Star System

Don't sell duplicate Pals — use the Pal Condenser. Four identical Pals combine into one with a higher star rating. A 4-star Pal operates roughly 30% faster than a fresh catch on all work tasks and combat stats. Since catching multiples while farming is free, condensing is basically free power.

Fast Level Trick: Pair a high-level Pal with a low-level Pal in the Breeding Farm. The offspring inherits approximately 30% of the combined parental level sum — significantly faster than grinding levels in the field.

All 5 Tower Bosses

Five towers. Five bosses. Each one harder than the last and each with a specific exploit that makes them way more manageable. Don't grind levels — grind the right type counter and half the fight is already won.

General Tips Before Any Tower Run

Always pre-feed your Pals before entering — a hungry Pal fights at reduced efficiency. Bring at least 10 High-Grade Medical Kits. If you have access to elemental buff potions (like Fire Boost Tonic), use them on your counter-type Pals — the damage difference is noticeable. And remember: you dying is fine, losing your Pal to a knockout is not. Keep your Pal healthy; burn through your own healing items freely.

Tower 1 — Zoe & Grizzbolt
⚡ Grizzbolt
Type: ElectricWeak to: GroundBest counter: Digtoise / LeafmothRecommended Level: 15–20

The first and most forgiving Tower Boss. Grizzbolt spams a three-hit ground-smash combo with a sizeable AoE — dodge-roll through all three and punish right after the third hit. The arena has pillars you can use as cover from Zoe's laser. Stick your Ground-type Pal on it for double-damage and this fight ends in minutes. Cattiva also works well here — its rapid-fire attacks strip the shield quickly.

Tower 2 — Lily & Lyleen
🌿 Lyleen
Type: GrassWeak to: FireBest counter: Foxspark / BlazamutRecommended Level: 20–30

Located in the snow region, so come equipped with cold-resist gear before you even reach the tower entrance. Inside, temperatures normalize. Lyleen's vine-bind attack roots you in place for several seconds while she unloads a seed gatling — watch for green rings on the ground and roll out immediately. Fire-type attacks apply a Burning DoT that dramatically shortens the fight. Flying mounts dramatically increase dodge mobility here — bring one if you have it.

Preparation Note: The surrounding snow biome deals continuous Cold damage. You need cold-resist armor and food before approaching. Equipping a fire torch in your off-hand works in a pinch.
Tower 3 — Axel & Orserk
🐉⚡ Orserk (Dragon/Electric)
Type: Dragon + ElectricWeak to: Ground + Ice (4× from Ice!)Best counter: Frostallion / Ice KingpacaRecommended Level: 35–40

Located in the volcano zone — mandatory heat-resist armor and a heat-resist accessory. The lava pools around the arena are a cheese opportunity: kite Orserk near the edge and bait its charge attack. It frequently charges straight into the lava, taking continuous burn damage while you pelt it from range. Its Thunderstorm covers roughly half the arena — run to the farthest edge the moment you see the charge-up animation. The very edge is safe.

Tower 4 — Marcus & Faleris
🔥 Faleris
Type: FireWeak to: Ice, WaterBest counter: Ice Kingpaca / Kelpsea IgnisRecommended Level: 40–45

The desert zone has extreme day/night temperature swings — you'll need both heat-resist and cold-resist gear, or craft the late-game combo armor. Faleris goes airborne frequently and drops cascading fire bombs. When it lifts off, mount your flying Pal immediately — aerial combat makes dodging the bombing runs far easier. Its homing fireballs dodge with lateral rolls, not straight backward sprints. Bring an assault rifle or better. Ice potions on your Ice-type Pal push the damage ceiling noticeably.

Tower 5 — Victor & Shadowbeak
🌑 Shadowbeak
Type: DarkWeak to: DragonBest counter: Jetragon / Elphidran / OrserkRecommended Level: 45–50

The hardest fight in the game. Shadowbeak has over 200,000 HP — bring a rocket launcher and don't hold back on ammo. The arena is small and its dark-pool spew leaves persistent damage zones that shrink your safe space constantly. Its dash attack is near-teleport speed — you won't predict it, just dodge laterally on instinct. When you see "Dark Nova" charging (a large black orb forming in the center of the arena), sprint to a pillar and get behind it before the explosion. Bring Witch or Lyleen for continuous team healing, and stack High-Grade Medical Kits. Plan on multiple attempts.

Post-Boss Rewards

Defeating each Tower Boss awards Ancient Technology Points for unlocking high-tier facilities like the Egg Incubator, electrical grid, and automated production structures. Additionally, after you beat a boss, that faction's field units turn friendly — you can now buy from their traders in the open world. Don't ignore this: some factions sell materials and Pal Spheres that are hard to source otherwise.

Rare Materials Farming Routes

Certain materials gate your entire mid-to-late game progression and can feel impossible to stockpile. Here's exactly where to farm each one efficiently — no more aimless wandering.

Pal Fluid

Required for cement, high-quality hot springs, and several armor sets — and merchants don't sell it. Your early source is Pengullet clusters along the coast below the starting spawn, where they appear in groups at level 10 or so. Mid-game, Surfent populations along the shoreline are higher density. The single most efficient farm spot is around coordinates (150, −209), where 20–30 Sharkids cluster in one area — a full loop drops 20+ Pal Fluid per run.

Leather

The core material for saddles and armor. Head to the "Twin Knights Bridge" fast travel point — the surrounding area spawns dense packs of Direhowl, almost every one of which drops leather. Earlier in the game, Foxspark and Surfent drop it at lower rates, but the Direhowl zone is 5× more efficient once you unlock the travel point.

Coal & Sulfur

Both are exclusive to the Volcano region and both require a Mining Lv2+ Pal to harvest (Digtoise or better). The most coal-dense node cluster is directly beside the Self-Defense Force Tower in the north desert — seven or eight node clusters in a tight area. Sulfur nodes are concentrated around the volcano crater itself. Come prepared with max heat-resist gear; you'll be there for a while.

High-Quality Pal Oil

Needed for Polymer, which feeds into almost every advanced crafting recipe. The fastest source is Dumud (a big slow fish Pal) in the desert — high drop rate, moves slowly, easy to farm in bulk. Woolipop also drops it but spawns in smaller numbers. The late-game solution is the Oil Well structure in the Snow region — it produces Raw Oil automatically, which you process into High-Quality Pal Oil at the Oil Refinery. Unlock it once you hit the relevant tech tier and never farm Dumud again.

Ancient Civilization Parts

The gating material for Ancient Tech items like the Egg Incubator and advanced glider. These only drop from World Bosses (the giant map icons) and dungeon treasure chests. Every World Boss respawns daily. Early on, farm Mammorest repeatedly — it's high level but slow-moving and burns for massive damage. Dungeons also award them from chests, but each dungeon requires a key to enter.

Pure Crystal

Required for Circuit Boards, high-tier shields, and glass components. Exclusively from Crystal Vein nodes in the Snow Tundra — they look like translucent blue formations and need a Mining Lv2+ Pal. Densest cluster is near the summit around Tower 5. Full cold-resist loadout mandatory; the summit is the coldest point on the map.

Pal Spheres — The Foxspark Method

Early-game you craft spheres manually, which burns through ore fast. The smarter long-term play: catch a Foxspark and put it in your Ranch. It produces Pal Spheres passively, including a small percentage of higher-quality balls. A 4-star Foxspark has roughly a 10% chance to produce Large Pal Spheres per production cycle. Getting one set up early saves hundreds of ore units over the course of a playthrough.

Pal Sphere Priority: The Foxspark ranch method alone eliminates one of the biggest mid-game resource drains. Even a 1-star Foxspark in the Ranch returns dividends faster than you'd expect — upgrade it as you find duplicates.
